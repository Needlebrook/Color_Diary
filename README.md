# 🌈 Color Diary

> *A Mood Journal with a Splash of Color*
<img width="496" height="245" alt="Screenshot 2025-09-15 210439" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/dd763a63-4857-4e7a-9ab5-a56d7430fc47" />

Color Diary is a simple & intuitive desktop app that lets you record your moods and daily reflections through colours. 
Each day gets its own color-coded entry, turning your days into a visual timeline of your emotions.

<img width="1160" height="855" alt="Screenshot 2025-09-14 155130" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/611273ef-31d6-4ac5-8624-5b692349fc2c" />

Built with Java Swing and SQLite, it prioritizes a minimalist presentation throughout. It provides tools for mood tracking as well as statistics & live weather updates on your city.

Through this project, we wanted to design a tool that encourages self-reflection without feeling heavy or clinical. 

## Details
Clicking on any day opens a dedicated entry window where users can record their mood rating (on a scale of 1–10), select an expressive emoji, and write a brief diary note about their experiences. All entries are stored in an integrated database, implemented in SQLite. 
The application also offers a statistics dashboard that presents analytical insights such as monthly mood averages, trend graphs, and emotional fluctuations over time. Additionally, a weather window allows users to view real-time local weather information 
(temperature, rainfall, and conditions) providing some context that might influence mood.

Each calendar day is shaded in a color representing the user’s mood rating, ranging from bright red for low moods to deep green for highly positive days. Users can personalize their experience by choosing from multiple color 
palettes such as Heatmap, Ocean, or Candy which offers visually distinct representations of mood variation throughout the month.

The system also includes a login feature, allowing each user to securely access their personal diary and maintain privacy. Once logged in, the interface opens to an intuitive calendar view where users can easily 
navigate between months and years to reflect on past emotional patterns.
